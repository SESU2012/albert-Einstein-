Life and Achievements

Einstein's contributions to science revolutionized our understanding of the universe. His famous equation, E=mc^2, demonstrates the relationship between energy and mass. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

Legacy

Albert Einstein's work laid the foundation for many scientific advancements and continues to inspire researchers and students worldwide. His insights into the nature of space, time, and energy have had a profound impact on the field of physics.
